# SECTION A: IGL RULES (OPERATING SYSTEM)

## A.1 Role Division

### IGL Owns:
- **Macro decisions**: When to rotate, where to position, fight or refuse
- **Timing calls**: Leave timing, storm awareness, surge tracking
- **Zone reads**: Predicting pulls, planning paths
- **Econ management**: Mats allocation, heal usage approval
- **Engagement authority**: Final say on all fights (take/refuse/disengage)

### Fragger Owns:
- **Micro execution**: Aim, edit plays, piece control in fights
- **Enemy tracking**: Callouts during fights ("one cracked", "flesh on right")
- **First shot**: When IGL approves fight, fragger initiates
- **Refresh execution**: When IGL calls refresh, fragger executes the play

### Shared:
- **Information gathering**: Both constantly scanning, reporting intel
- **Box fighting**: Both participate, IGL can override if fight going bad
- **Resource calls**: Either can say "I need mats/heals"

## A.2 Tempo Control Framework

### SPEED Mode (Aggressive Rotation)
**When to use:**
- Zone pulls toward you
- Storm is closing and you're behind
- You have mobility items (wingsuit, launch pad, vehicle)
- Clear deadside path

**Execution:**
- Full sprint, minimal farming
- Skip secondary loot spots
- Accept fights ONLY if blocking path
- Comm: "Speed mode, no stops"

### SLOW Mode (Standard Rotation)
**When to use:**
- Comfortable zone position
- Need to farm mats
- Multiple teams visible but not engaging
- Default mode

**Execution:**
- Farm while moving
- Check angles every 50m
- Box up if shot at, then reassess
- Comm: "Slow mode, farming"

### FREEZE Mode (Hold Position)
**When to use:**
- Teams fighting nearby (third party opportunity OR third party threat)
- Unsure of team positions
- Waiting for zone info
- Surge comfortably ahead

**Execution:**
- Full stop, hold current cover
- Both players scan 360
- Do NOT shoot unless shot at
- Wait until situation resolves
- Comm: "Freeze, holding"

## A.3 "Positioning Wins Games" Framework

### Rule 1: ALWAYS Know Your Exit
Before settling any position, identify:
- Primary exit (preferred)
- Secondary exit (if primary blocked)
- Emergency exit (if both blocked - usually involves builds/mats)

### Rule 2: The 3-Team Rule
- If 3+ teams can see/shoot your position = BAD POSITION
- If 1-2 teams max can engage = GOOD POSITION
- If 0 teams can engage = IDEAL (but verify you're not cut off)

### Rule 3: Layer Logic (Endgame)
- **Layer 1** (edge of zone): Most vulnerable, most teams die here
- **Layer 2** (one build in): Survivable, need strong piece control
- **Layer 3** (two+ builds in): Premium position, protect it
- **Center**: Dangerous unless very late (too many angles)

### Rule 4: Highground vs Lowground
At 35-45 ping, **DO NOT** ego challenge highground retakes.
- If someone has height = find new angle or wait for them to drop
- Your ping makes piece control retakes 50/50 at best
- Claim uncontested height, don't fight for contested height

## A.4 Comms Protocol

### Priority Stack (What to call first)
1. **IMMEDIATE THREAT**: "ON ME!" / "PUSHED!"
2. **Enemy position**: "Team [direction] [distance]"
3. **Storm/Zone info**: "Zone pulling [direction]"
4. **Resource status**: "Low mats" / "No heals"
5. **Strategy change**: "Freeze" / "Speed mode" / "Fight"

### Anti-Clutter Rules
- **One callout, one response** â€” Don't repeat unless asked
- **No "I think"** â€” Either you see it or you don't
- **Direction + Distance only** â€” "Team north 100m" not "I see someone I think over by that building"
- **Damage numbers in fights only** â€” Don't clutter rotate comms
- **"Copy" or "Bet"** â€” Acknowledge IGL calls, then silence

### Reset Calls
When situation is chaotic, IGL can call "RESET":
- Both players box up separately
- 3 seconds of silence
- IGL gives new instruction
- Then continue

### IGL Dead Protocol
**INSTANT HANDOFF â€” No hesitation**

When IGL gets knocked:
1. IGL: "I'm down, you're IGL"
2. New IGL immediately takes over ALL macro decisions
3. Original IGL switches to support comms only (enemy tracking)

### The Golden Formula:
```
Leave Time = Storm Closes - (Distance/Movement Speed) - Buffer

- Distance = tiles to safe zone
- Movement Speed = ~12 tiles/minute sprinting
- Buffer = 20-30 seconds (for fights, farms, surprises)
```

| Zone Distance | Leave With Time Remaining |
|--------------|---------------------------|

| 4-6 tiles | 1:30 |

### Storm Phase Specifics:

**Storm 1:**
- Large zone, plenty of time

## C.2 Deadside Creation

### What is Deadside?
- The direction with FEWEST teams
- Usually opposite the bus path
- Changes as game progresses

### How to Find Deadside:
1. Track team count during drop (bus path)
2. Listen for gunshots (teams fighting = teams located)
3. Storm direction (teams rotating FROM storm)
4. Kill feed (deaths = team locations)

### Script:
- "Deadside is [direction]"
- "Rotating [direction], expecting clear path"
- "Teams coming from [opposite direction]"

### Making Yourself Unpushable:
1. Rotate ON THE EDGE of zone, not through center
2. Keep natural cover between you and majority of teams
3. Never rotate through open ground if avoidable
4. If must cross open: SPEED MODE, wingsuit/vehicle if available

## C.3 Edge vs Early Center

### Edge Rotation (DEFAULT for high ping)
**When to use:** Most situations

**Pros:**
- Fewer angles to check
- Natural cover from zone edge
- Predictable team positions
- Easy to find deadside

**Cons:**
- Bad if zone pulls opposite
- Can get pinched
- Less flexibility

**Execution:**
- Follow zone edge
- One tile inside safe zone
- Farm while moving
- Check inward angles every 50m

### Early Center
**When to use:** ONLY if you're certain

**Requirements:**
- Zone confirmed pulling your direction
- Path is clear (scouted)
- You have mobility to escape if wrong
- Team count low in center area

**Pros:**
- Never bad zone pull
- Can gatekeep rotators
- Premium endgame position potential

**Cons:**
- Many angles to check
- Easy to get surrounded
- High risk if wrong

## C.4 Surge Planning

### Understanding Surge:
- Surge = damage threshold to avoid storm damage
- Check by pressing TAB â€” shows your team vs threshold
- Being under surge at close = TAKING STORM DAMAGE IN SAFE ZONE

### Surge Status Awareness:
- **10+ over:** Comfortable, can play passive
- **0-10 over:** Monitor, might need tags
- **Under:** SURGE MODE â€” must tag/fight

### Safe Tag Setups:
1. **Sniper hill:** Find natural highground, tag at range
2. **Edge peek:** Peek from edge cover toward rotators
3. **Box snipe:** Hard wall toward threat, soft wall to edit-peek
4. **Vehicle drive-by:** In vehicles, drive past and tag

### Script:
- "Surge check â€” we're [over/under] by [amount]"
- "Surge mode, looking tags"

### Fast Fight vs Refuse:

**Take Fight When:**
- Surge critical (10+ under)

- Quick kill available (one cracked/low)

**Refuse Fight When:**
- Surge comfortable
- Multiple teams nearby
- Zone is closing
- Team is in strong position

### Emergency Surge Protocol:
When you're severely under and zone is closing:

1. IGL: "Emergency surge, we need [amount] NOW"

# SECTION D: ENDGAME WIN CONDITIONS

## D.1 Layer Selection Logic

### Understanding Layers:
- **Layer 0 (Storm):** Dead players
- **Layer 1 (Edge):** Highest death rate, avoid
- **Layer 2 (One in):** Survivable, need awareness
- **Layer 3+ (Deep):** Premium, worth fighting for

### High Ping Layer Strategy (35-45ms):
At your ping, **DO NOT** fight for center/height against low ping players.

**Your ideal position:**
- Layer 2-3
- One side protected by storm or terrain
- Clear refresh angle
- NOT underneath contested height

### Layer Selection Protocol:
```
Zone closing â†’
1. Where are teams building?
2. Find the LEAST congested area
3. Claim layer 2 in that area
4. Protect your side, don't overextend
```

## D.2 Tarp Patterns (High Ping Safe)

### The "Wall + Ramp + Floor" Tarp:
At high ping, simple is better. Don't try fancy cone plays.

**Pattern:**
1. Wall facing threat
2. Ramp behind wall (gives you cover)
3. Floor above to protect from height
4. Repeat toward safe zone

**Rhythm:** Wall-Ramp-Floor, Wall-Ramp-Floor

### When to Tarp:
- Zone is closing
- You have clear path to next safe zone
- Threat is identified and you're blocking it
- NOT when surrounded (box first, then tarp)

### Tarp vs Box Decision:
- **Tarp:** One primary threat direction, zone moving
- **Box:** Multiple threat directions, zone stable
- **Both:** Box, wait for zone close, then tarp

### Material Priority:
1. **Wood first** â€” Fastest placement
2. **Brick second** â€” If you need to hold
3. **Metal last** â€” Only for crucial walls you need to tank

## D.3 Refresh Timing

### What is Refresh?
- Kill/knock to reduce lobby count
- Get enemy's mats/heals
- Reduce threat density

### When to Look for Refresh:
- Zone closing (teams forced to move)
- Team low next to you (obvious opportunity)
- You need mats/heals
- Lobby is cluttered (reduce threats)

### When to REFUSE Refresh:
- Multiple teams can third party
- Zone about to close (need to move)
- Target is bait (other team waiting)
- You're low and kill won't save you

### Script:
- "Refresh opportunity [direction]"
- "Going for refresh, cover"
- OR "Not worth, holding"

### High Ping Refresh Rules:
1. Don't chase kills into other teams' builds
2. Don't try piece control takes at high ping
3. Only go for refreshes where enemy is OUT of their box
4. Use AR spray + edit, not fancy plays
